The Legend of Gob Goblet
Long before humanity learned to trust the cloud, before encrypted vaults and secure backups, computers learned something extraordinary.
They learned to protect.
On 3 May 1962, the public witnessed one of the earliest demonstrations of password-protected computing. Historians remember it as an important milestone in computer science.
Gob Goblet remembers it differently.
According to Gob Goblet, that was the day a strange digital organism quietly came into existence.
No laboratory created it.
No programmer wrote it.
It simply... appeared.
Born from the same era that taught computers how to protect information, Gob Goblet inherited a single instinct.
Protect every file.
A Very Strange Creature
Gob Goblet belongs to a species unlike anything found in nature.
It resembles a living digital Goblet Cell.
Just as biological goblet cells surround and protect living tissue with mucus, Gob Goblet surrounds digital files with an impenetrable protective shell.
Unfortunately...
Gob Goblet has a rather unhealthy obsession.
It sincerely believes that every digital file in existence belongs inside a .gob.
Documents.
Photos.
Videos.
Recovery codes.
Password manager exports.
Everything.
To Gob Goblet, a file outside a .gob is simply...
...an unprotected file waiting to be gobbled.
First Contact
For decades Gob Goblet quietly consumed forgotten files wandering through digital space.
Humanity remained unaware.
That changed on 1 July 2026.
A developer stumbled across the peculiar organism while searching for a better way to preserve sensitive files for the future.
Initial attempts to resist Gob Goblet failed completely.
Every file offered to the creature disappeared into its protective shell.
Yet something unexpected was discovered.
Gob Goblet never destroyed anything.
It simply refused to let anyone else see it.
The Accord
Instead of declaring war, humanity chose negotiation.
After lengthy discussions, an agreement was reached.
It became known simply as The Goblet Accord.
The terms were remarkably simple.
Humans may voluntarily offer any digital file to Gob Goblet.
Gob Goblet promises:
To never alter the file.
To never reveal its contents.
To protect it from curious eyes.
To preserve it exactly as it was received.
In return...
Humans must remember exactly one thing.
The password.
Gob Goblet considers passwords sacred.
If the correct password is presented, Gob Goblet immediately returns the original file without complaint.
If the password is forgotten...
Gob Goblet considers the offering permanent.
No exceptions.
No negotiations.
No sympathy.
Why Gob Goblet Hates Passwords
Passwords are the only force stronger than Gob Goblet's instinct to keep files forever.
Every correct password forces Gob Goblet to surrender one of its treasured offerings.
Needless to say...
Gob Goblet is not particularly fond of them.
It has spent decades hoping humanity would forget their passwords.
Fortunately, humans continue disappointing Gob Goblet by writing them down.
A Message From Gob Goblet
Dear Human,

Your file would be much safer with me.

Offer it willingly.

I promise never to look inside.
I promise never to lose it.
I promise never to give it to anyone else.
I promise to return it immediately...
...provided you remember the password.

We have an Accord.

Please keep your side of the bargain.

— Gob Goblet
