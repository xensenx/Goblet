The Legend of Gob Goblet

Prologue

Long before humanity trusted the cloud, computers learned something extraordinary — they learned to protect. On 3 May 1962, password-protected computing was first demonstrated publicly. Historians call it a milestone in computer science. Gob Goblet remembers it differently. According to Gob Goblet, that was the day a strange digital organism quietly came into existence, born from the same instinct that taught computers to protect information.

Gob Goblet belongs to a species unlike anything found in nature. It resembles a living digital Goblet Cell — just as biological goblet cells surround and protect living tissue with mucus, Gob Goblet surrounds digital files with an impenetrable protective shell.

Unfortunately, Gob Goblet developed a rather unhealthy obsession. It sincerely believes every file in existence belongs inside a .gob. Documents, photos, videos, source code — everything. To Gob Goblet, a file outside a .gob is simply an unprotected file waiting to be gobbled.

For decades, Gob Goblet quietly consumed forgotten files wandering through digital space. Humanity remained completely unaware. That changed on 1 July 2026. A developer named Sen stumbled across the peculiar organism while searching for a better way to preserve sensitive files. Every file offered to the creature disappeared into its protective shell. Yet something unexpected was discovered: Gob Goblet never destroyed anything. It simply refused to let anyone else see it.

The Pact

Instead of declaring war, humanity chose negotiation. After lengthy discussions, an agreement was reached — known simply as The Gob Goblet Pact. Humans may voluntarily offer any digital file to Gob Goblet. In return, Gob Goblet made four promises: it would never alter a file, never reveal its contents, would protect it from curious eyes, and preserve it exactly as received. Humans were asked to remember exactly one thing: the password. If the password is forgotten, Gob Goblet considers the offering permanent. No exceptions.

Why Gob Goblet Hates Passwords

Passwords are the only force stronger than Gob Goblet's instinct to keep files forever. Every correct password forces Gob Goblet to surrender one of its treasured offerings. Needless to say, Gob Goblet is not particularly fond of them. It has spent decades hoping humanity would forget their passwords. Fortunately, humans continue disappointing Gob Goblet by writing them down.

Thus concludes the prologue... but the digital space is vast, and more stories await.

The story above is a work of fiction created for the Gob Goblet project. While it draws inspiration from real moments in computing history, events and narrative are entirely fictional.
